Will you still Love me when I’m no longer Young and Beautiful

Sep 24 2022 3 min read

Lana Del Rey once said, “Will you still love me when I’m no longer a minor, and everything I do is expected of me, and I have a hard time adapting to adulthood?”

I’m still a Minor (haha, little boy), so there are some things I foresee but not experiencing.

I am well aware that everything I do from now until November 12 has a seeming “boost” because I’m not 18, but what happens when I am 18?

What happens when my competition pool goes from tik-tok-addicted teenagers to young adults who have already dedicated themselves to their field or craft?

What happens when I apply for the same job as someone with 4 years more experience, 4 years more of school, 2 more startups than me while being willing to work for Pennies just because they want to work for MAANG, some famous news network, or Tesla/Space X?

What happens to my fragile little brain when I go from being praised for taking a shower and waking up at 5:30 every day to getting clowned for not being published on an aCaDeMiC journal or not having had experience interning for 60hrs/week at a Fortune 500 company?

Eh, whatever. I know it’ll take some adapting. I just wanted to exaggerate a little bit.

I’m seeing this quarter-life crisis from miles away, and I’m getting ready for it.

Will I be 100% ready when it comes?

Heck no.

Will I be shocked when it happens?

Heck no.

I have to keep taking advantage of the advantage I have now.

I am so early on in life that wherever I set my aim drastically changes the final destination.

Am I getting better at writing?

No.

Am I liking writing more?

Yes.

Ethan Castro

Some random dude who is somehow having an effective gap year.
