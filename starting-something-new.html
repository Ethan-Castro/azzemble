Starting Something New

Sep 4 2022 2 min read

When starting something new, sometimes you feel like a complete idiot, worthless, weak, incapable, and whatever else.

My most monumental beginnings were: my first year at Brooklyn Tech (my High School), my first season of Wrestling (my first & most excruciating sport), and now into Programming.

It is emasculating to be double-legged or suplexed so hard that you feel like you got winded, lost IQ points, and broke your neck all at the same time… by someone 30 lbs less than you.

It is demoralizing to go from number 1 in your school from 4–14 years old and having that be your identity, to then go into High School and be 1/6000 kids that were also number one; pushing you down to being #2000/6000 if you’re somewhat decent.

But, I’ve already had those experiences and at least somewhat managed to surpass that beginning stage of adaptation.

So now, going into programming after telling myself the last four years that I’d never need it and I’ll just be a consultant or whatever other scapegoat answer my conscious brain gave my worried subconscious, I know what to expect.

Right now, I feel pretty damn dumb. But I am competent (or experienced) enough to know this feeling is temporary.

At least looking at a foreign line of code for the first time doesn’t hurt as bad as getting caught in a rear naked choke was the first time.

Ethan Castro

Some random dude who is somehow having an effective gap year.
