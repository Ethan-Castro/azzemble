AI Attack Against Artists. SM(ART)

Oct 4 2022 3 min read

Good News: AI can create art now.

Bad News: It’s good at it.

Here are some examples

Those are pretty insane, but here are some pieces of ‘AI Art’ from other people online

This realism is uncanny and a bit worrisome.

This could make you think about a few things:

What will happen to artists?

Is this actually art?

If AI can do this, what will it be able to do in the future?

Let’s focus on the second question: Is this actually art?

On top of the fact that I am not yet advanced enough to give a quality answer to the other questions, this question seems to be the most debated now.

Twitter is currently blowing up with debates on whether AI art is ‘Soulless” or not.

On the one hand, it seems illogical to accept what a code created to be art when all that was done was type a few descriptive words into a box to output some drawing.

On the other hand, art is art. Beauty is in the eye of the beholder, and some things are so aesthetically pleasing that the beauty of it is almost objective and not up for debate.

I don’t know what to make of it. The most accurate perspective I can make is to imagine AI overtaking the ‘artistic endeavors’ that I enjoy, whether writing, talking, or recording.

I have played around with some AI that finishes sentences, makes stories, creates dialogue, and creates short videos.

The only reason I am semi-accepting of it is that I am more on the side of an appreciator than a creator. I can look at work created by AI without feeling offended or in danger. I appreciate what this could mean for the advancement of art, but I also fear what this could mean for the future of art.

Is the sacrifice of Better Art for Less Artists a reasonable one? (If you even consider it to be better)

Are we willing to outsource all artists to programmers who are the only ones who know how to program the APIs for the AI?

Are we willing to outsource art to ‘computers’?

Can living artists and AI artists coexist?
