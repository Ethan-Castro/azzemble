Random Words

Sep 18 2022 3 min read

Sup fellas,

I write this little piece at 4:35 am, the morning of my bodybuilding competition.

Things have been going well.

After weeks of resistance that my brain gave me towards wanting to quit coding, I finally reached that ‘summit.’ I actually get it to an extent and I am starting to even like it.

Every day it has seemingly gotten easier after those first few weeks.

From getting annoyed that I couldn’t grasp even the most beginner Codecademy courses to then, in just a few days, making data entries on excel for my data visualizations on tableau, to using python to make other visuals and to code my own cryptocurrency token on the Ethereum blockchain, to now writing my own blog site with CSS & HTML. I’m pretty serious about this.

The baristas I love talking to at Starbucks think I’m a nerd because I get there at 6:30 am and don’t take my eyes off of my laptop until 1:30 (although I do love taking 20 min breaks to have hilarious convos with them).

The moms on the treadmills at the gym probably think the gym is all I have going for me because they see me every day… sometimes twice.

The other practitioners in my jiu-jitsu gym probably don’t think twice.

The deli near my gym, where I get 6 hard-boiled eggs a day, probably just likes my money.

I don’t even know who/how I truly am. I change so frequently that I try not to even think about it anymore. I just use my efforts to strive to be the person in which I want to be. The more I think about how I am in the moment, the more self-conscious or narcissistic I get.

I hope all is well.

Ethan Castro

Some random dude who is somehow having an effective gap year.
