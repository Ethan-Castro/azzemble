Falling in Love with Learning

Sep 24 2022 3 min read

Today while I was cleaning out the Adidas backpack with the broken buckle that I used to use every day for school, I was injected with a sense of rage and sadness.

I have fallen in love with learning. I jump for joy when my code works after having resisted punching my computer for being slow for the last hour. I get an unhealthily high dopamine hit when I update my little linktree on my new project. I feel accomplished when I understand how something that was once foreign works. The more I learn, the more I want to learn. The more things work, the more I want to work.

I was once like this when I was very young, and that love for learning peaked in about fourth grade and carried me literally to the end of high school.

Sometimes I have [truthful] intrusive thoughts that replay over and over…

If only I had done my HOMEWORK ON TIME, I would’ve already been in the college I wanted, instead of going to Starbucks at 6:30 am every morning and having to learn entirely new subjects.

If only I cared when it mattered, I wouldn’t be in this position where I question everything about myself and my value.

Today I was cleaning out my backpack, and I saw

  • A Calc test that said, “Come to tutoring, this is the third test you messed up.”
  • An English assignment that I wrote in the worst penmanship that someone could still find somewhat legible
  • A crumbled stats cheat sheet that literally had everything I needed in that class

For a split second, I gave myself permission to be regretful and frustrated at Ethan from 6 months ago. Though, I’ve come to realize you must learn to consciously choose to think through a logical lens rather than an emotional one because sometimes the only emotions that present themself are toxic ones.

After draining the flood of emotions, I realized something;

I LOVE LEARNING AGAIN.

When my friends text me all their homework or assignments, I respond with, “That’s so fire.” I don’t think they realize I’d kill to have homework right now. To have someone else literally tell me what to do, me do it, hand it to them, they tell me how I did, and all of this for me to learn a subject better and make more money down the line.

Without this doggone gap year, I wouldn’t have fallen in love with learning.

I guess I could say thank you to my (6 months ago) younger self for having his priorities wrong and being lazy.

I promise I will be the happiest student on the campus of whatever college decides to take a chance on me.

Ethan Castro

Some random dude who is somehow having an effective gap year.
