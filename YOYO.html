You're Only Young Once - YOYO

Oct 7 2022 2 min read

Listen, guys, you get one life, and it’s not very long.

There is a famous remark older folks love to make (they really love saying this), here it is: “Youth is Wasted on the Young.”

Older folks wish they could go back into their vibrant and vivacious bodies with the expertise, experience, revelations, and realizations they have acquired over their years of living.

We get one attempt to do magnificent things or minuscule things, lead an admirable or average life, have a life of aspirations, or accept things as they are.

We can do incredible things that impact for years, or we can scroll through a box of light and admire the incredible things other people (seem to) do.

We can collectively scroll through the art galleries of celebrities or celebrate the life we are so blessed to have by creating our own masterpieces.

The choice is yours.

Both sides have their tradeoffs, and your priorities will determine your best path.

I hope I can provide clarification for you on the choice to make.

We can look back on life and wish we would’ve done more with the opportunities we had — the energy, freedom, health, friends, risk tolerance, and everything we had while in our prime — after an existential crisis at an older age.

Or, we can look back and cherish all the times we worked while others went out to play — we were cringed at for being try-hards, we resisted the urge to scroll hours, we picked up a book instead of our phone, or we went to work to build capital to fund our projects while others were chillin’ — while eating the fruits of our labor.
