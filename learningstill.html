Still Learning

Sep 24 2022 3 min read

I know that I know barely anything.
— Ethan (not Socrates)

I am a teenager who’s been validated and recognized for being a smarty pants his whole life, so I am not yet at a point where I know that I know nothing because my ego is still too strong.

I do know that I don’t know anywhere near as much as I once thought I did.

Every day I am intellectually checked.

It’s usually incited by the fact that I am nowhere near the position I thought I would be in at this moment in time.

After careful reflection every day, I get closer and closer to a complete internalization that I do not know how the world works, how life works, how progression works, how hard work works, how discipline works, or how socializing works.

I am just winging it. When I try to plan, I never take into consideration the fact that life isn’t linear, that progress doesn’t always look like a progression, that emotions aren’t planned, and that just about everything you do/want to do is conditional on something else or someone else doing something to allow you to do that something you plan on doing.

All beginner workout plans go like this:

  • Week 1: 5 Pushups
  • Week 2: 7 Pushups
  • Week 3: 9 Pushups

At this rate, you should be doing 100 pushups by the end of the year.

Is that how it works? No.

And that’s how everything goes.

You get sick, distracted, and lost, and you realize that you had no clue what you were doing when you made the plan.

I am unsure if the main lesson here is

Stay Flexible While Keeping Your Aim on the End Goal.

or

Accept that you know Nothing and Seek Guidance from those with Experience.

My advice to all of my friends who identify as a smarty pants is to journey to the realization that you know nothing or nowhere near as much as you once thought. Also, realize that no one knows what they’re doing; some people have good pattern recognition after years of trial and error and were able to swallow their pride and seek Guidance when they were younger.

Do you think your parents were expert parents when you were born

No, but they did it.

Do you think your math teacher has the most excellent pedagogical philosophy?

No, but you still learned y=mx+b.

Do you think your doctor knows every possible medical diagnosis?

No, but he’s still saved many lives.

Do you think I know how to write a masterful article?

HELL NO! But I still won’t shut up.

Ethan Castro

Some random dude who is somehow having an effective gap year.
